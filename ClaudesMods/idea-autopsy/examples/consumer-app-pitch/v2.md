# GigPurse — Budgeting for the 1099 economy (v2)

## The problem

Rideshare drivers have lumpy, unpredictable weekly income but rigid monthly expenses (car payment, insurance, rent). Existing budgeting apps are built around a steady W-2 paycheck and a monthly calendar. Drivers we interviewed describe "feeling guilty" using them — the apps assume a job they don't have.

## The solution

GigPurse is a mobile-first budgeting app for variable-income workers. The product centers on a **weekly Sunday-evening habit loop:** a text that shows the user "$X buffer, M weeks of expenses covered" before they choose how hard to work the next week. Three supporting features:

1. **Weekly rolling buffer** (the habit hook) — runway in weeks, not month-over-month spending.
2. **Auto-categorized rideshare income** — Plaid detects Uber/Lyft payouts and separates them from tips, bonuses, and side income.
3. **Tax-set-aside autopilot** — federal+state set-aside transferred to a sub-account on every payout. Launching with CA, TX, NY, FL, IL; other states get manual-mode with a banner.

## Market

**Beachhead:** US rideshare drivers earning ≥50% of income from gig work. Population: ~2M (BLS Dec 2025 + LFS supplements). SAM at 5% adoption × $8/mo × 12 = $9.6M annual.

We expand to delivery (DoorDash, Instacart) after rideshare validates, then to creative gig workers. The "$1.7B TAM" calc from v1 was top-down and not addressable; we cut it.

**Load-bearing assumption:** 5% adoption requires Mint-comparable conversion in a more pain-aligned cohort. If conversion is < 1%, the rideshare beachhead alone is too small to justify the raise.

## Business model

Freemium. Free tier: budgeting + rideshare-income categorization. Pro tier ($8/mo): tax autopilot, weekly buffer, multi-account.

**LTV / CAC sanity:** at 15% annual churn, LTV ≈ $55. We require blended CAC ≤ $18 to clear 3× LTV/CAC.

Side revenue (year 2+): referral fees from tax-prep at year-end.

## Traction (paid-pilot data, not projections)

We ran a 60-day paid pilot with 22 waitlist members at $8/mo:
- **18/22 paid for month 1.** 4 didn't enter payment info.
- **15/22 paid for month 2.** 3 churned (cited "didn't open the app enough").
- **Top-used feature by usage minutes:** tax set-aside (78% of paid users opened it weekly).
- **Average buffer-text open rate:** 64% on Sunday evenings.

This is signal that drivers will pay; we do not yet have signal on retention beyond 60 days or on paid-acquisition CAC.

## Distribution

**Primary channel commitment for the first 12 months:** TikTok creator partnerships with mid-tier rideshare-driver creators (5k–50k followers). Test budget: $25k over 6 months. Target CAC: ≤$18 (matches LTV constraint).

We are NOT betting on Reddit, organic word-of-mouth, or partnership deals as the primary channel. If TikTok-CAC test fails, the v3 will be paused for an acquisition-channel re-strategy.

## Operational scope (launch)

- **Tax states supported at launch:** CA, TX, NY, FL, IL. Other states: manual-mode with banner explaining set-aside calculation.
- **Plaid COGS:** $0.50/connection/month. At 5k users: $2,500/mo.
- **Support staffing:** founders for first 12 months. March/April support spike (~4× normal volume) acknowledged; budget includes 1 contracted support hire for tax season.
- **User off-ramp:** 1-click CSV export of all data; account closure deletes within 30 days. Documented in onboarding.
