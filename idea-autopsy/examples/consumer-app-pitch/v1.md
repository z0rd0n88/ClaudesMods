# GigPurse — Budgeting for the 1099 economy

## The problem

Gig workers (Uber, DoorDash, Instacart, freelance creatives) have lumpy, unpredictable income but rigid monthly expenses. Existing budgeting apps (Mint, YNAB, Copilot) are built around a steady W-2 paycheck and a monthly calendar. They make gig workers feel like they're failing at budgeting when they're actually just doing a different job.

## The solution

GigPurse is a mobile-first budgeting app built for variable income. Three differentiating features:

1. **Weekly rolling buffer** — instead of "you spent $X this month," we show "your runway is N weeks at current pace." Workers think in weeks, not months.
2. **Auto-categorized gig income** — bank-account connection (Plaid) detects Uber/DoorDash/Instacart payouts and tags them as gig income vs. side income vs. tips, so workers can see which platforms are paying.
3. **Tax-set-aside autopilot** — every time gig income hits the account, the app calculates the federal+state set-aside, transfers it to a separate sub-account, and shows the worker their true take-home.

## Market

There are ~70M gig workers in the US (per the BLS). 25% of them earn >50% of their income from gig work — that's our addressable segment, ~18M people. At $8/month, that's a $1.7B TAM if we capture even 1% of the segment.

## Business model

Freemium. Free tier: basic budgeting + gig-income categorization. Pro tier ($8/mo): tax autopilot, weekly buffer view, multi-account, export-to-CSV for tax season.

Side revenue: referral fees from tax-prep software (TurboTax, FreeTaxUSA) at year-end.
